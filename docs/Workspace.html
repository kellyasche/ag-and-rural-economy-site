Peak farm earnings vs. peak total earnings


County maps - change in earnings
from peak farm years

This map helps us identify which counties have experienced the most significant decreases in total earnings along with seeing where some of the most serious decreases in farm earnings. Not suprisingly, some of the largest decreases in total earnings has occured in counties in the southwestern and western parts of the state. The percent decrease in farm earnings from the peak farm year to 2018 isn’t as helpful. The map shows that the largest decreases in farm earnings has occured in north-central Minnesota. However, hovering over those counties shows that although the percentage decreases are high, the actual amount of money isn’t as much since agriculture is such a small piece of the economy. For example, in 2018 Itasca County made 132% less in farm earnings than it’s peak farming year of 2011. However, the actual dollar decrease is $4 million. On the flip side, Stevens County experienced only a 66% decrease in farm earnings, but the actual dollar decrease was $90 million.
